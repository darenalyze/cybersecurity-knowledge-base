## My Understanding
- (Video) — Stuxnet is the first worm malware ever made in history. It targets hardware to cause physical damage by manipulating the software that controls it. This kind of malware is perfect for cyberwarfare because attackers can target another nation's infrastructure without any physical contact. Stuxnet is also notably intelligent — unlike a virus, it does not require any user interaction to spread.
- The purpose of cyberwarfare is to gather information about the enemy's movements and strategies in order to counter them. This includes stealing defense secrets and acquiring knowledge about technology that helps narrow the gaps in military and industrial capabilities.
- Beyond industrial and military espionage, a nation can continuously attack another nation's infrastructure to cause disruption and chaos. For example, a cyberattack could shut down the power grid of an entire city.
