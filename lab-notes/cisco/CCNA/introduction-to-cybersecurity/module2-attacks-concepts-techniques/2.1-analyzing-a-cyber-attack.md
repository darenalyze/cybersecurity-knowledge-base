## My Understanding (In My Own Words)
 
### Types of Malware
 
- **Spyware**
  Designed to track and spy on you, spyware monitors your online activity and can log every key you press on your keyboard, as well as capture almost any of your data.
---
 
- **Adware**
  It shows unexpected pop-up ads on your device. This benefits the adware owner because they earn money depending on how many times their ads are displayed on a device.
---
 
- **Backdoor**
  A backdoor can access the root system. This is dangerous because the attacker can gain access without the user knowing what actions the backdoor is performing.
---
 
- **Ransomware**
  This malware encrypts your data inside your computer, and you can only unlock it if you pay the amount demanded by the hacker. Hackers require payment via cryptocurrency because it allows them to remain almost completely anonymous.
---
 
- **Scareware**
  It uses "scare" tactics to trick you into taking a specific action. Have you ever seen a message saying "Malware detected on your device," followed by "Download this app to remove the malware"? The app they want you to download is actually the real threat. They are scaring you so that you act quickly without even being aware of it.
---
 
- **Rootkit**
  It is a type of malware that hides itself, as well as other malware that comes with it. Because it runs at the root level of the system, some anti-malware programs are unable to detect it.
---
 
- **Virus**
  A virus is a self-spreading program. It attaches itself to executable files, and once you run an infected file, it can spread further every time those files are executed.
---
 
- **Trojan Horse**
  A Trojan Horse can be hard to recognize at first, because the program it comes bundled with appears to pose no threat at all — but once you execute it, it is often too late. Unlike viruses, Trojans do not replicate themselves instead, they act as a decoy to sneak malicious software past unsuspecting users.
---
 
- **Worms**
  Worms are like a smarter version of a virus because they can spread without any user input. They are capable of spreading through hardware such as USB drives, and they often carry out many more malicious actions beyond just spreading themselves.
---
 
### Symptoms of Malware
 
- Sudden increase in CPU usage
- PC crashing or freezing
- Unexplainable problems with your network
- Pop-ups of unknown files, programs, or desktop icons
- Modified or deleted files
