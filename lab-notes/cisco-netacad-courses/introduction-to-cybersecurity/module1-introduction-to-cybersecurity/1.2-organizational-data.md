## My Understanding
- Organizations can use your data to improve their system or strategies (it depends on what agreement between user and organization)
- Internet of Things (IoT) is a big network of physical objects, like sensors, software and other equipment. All of this is connected to the internet with the ability to collect and share data.

### The Cube (McCumber Cube)
> A simple but powerful framework **how to protect information** — not just what to protect, but where it is and how we're guarding it.
> It's called "The Cube" because it has **3 dimensions**, each with 3 parts making 27 possible combinations.

#### Dimension 1 — The CIA Triad (Core Principles)
Think of these as the **3 goals** of any security system.

| Principle | In plain words | How it's done |
|---|---|---|
| **Confidentiality** | Only the right people can see the data | Encryption, 2FA, identity verification |
| **Integrity** | Data stays accurate | Hash functions, checksums |
| **Availability** | Authorized users can access it when they need it | Backups, software updates, hardware maintenance |

#### Dimension 2 — States of Data

Data isn't always just "sitting there" — it moves around and changes form. Security has to follow it **everywhere**.

- **Processing** → data being actively used or changed *(data in process)*
- **Storage** → data resting on a drive or in memory *(data at rest)*
- **Transmission** → data traveling between systems *(data in transit)*

#### Dimension 3 — Safeguards

These are the actual **tools and methods** organizations use to protect information.

- **Awareness & Training** → teaching people to recognize threats and act safely
- **Technology** → hardware/software tools like firewalls that monitor and defend
- **Policy & Procedure** → the rules and plans (like incident response guides) that keep everything organized
---

- For me, phishing is one of the easiest and most common attacks in the cyber world because it allows them to choose a wide range of targets and use social engineering to gain a person's data.
- IoT is a perfect target to infect, in what is called "zombie bots". Hackers can use these "zombies" to generate traffic or flood a server or site, because of this the site they attack cannot be used by normal users. Organizations might get reputational damage, vandalism, theft, loss of revenue, and damage to intellectual property.
